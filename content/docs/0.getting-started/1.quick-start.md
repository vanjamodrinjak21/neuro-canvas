# Quick Start

This guide walks you through signing up, creating your first mind map, and learning the core interactions. You will have a working map in about five minutes.

## 1. Create an account

Open NeuroCanvas in your browser and click **Sign In** from the landing page.

You have three options:

- **Email and password** -- enter your email, choose a password, and verify via the confirmation link sent to your inbox.
- **Google OAuth** -- click "Continue with Google" to authenticate with your Google account.
- **GitHub OAuth** -- click "Continue with GitHub" to authenticate with your GitHub account.

After signing in, you land on the **Dashboard** -- your home base for managing maps.

::callout{type="info"}
On the Tauri desktop app, authentication is bypassed entirely. Maps are stored locally and you go straight to the dashboard on launch.
::

## 2. Create your first map

From the dashboard, you have several ways to start:

### Manual creation
Click the **+ New Map** button. NeuroCanvas creates a blank canvas with a single root node. Give your map a title by clicking the title area in the top bar.

### AI-generated map
Click **AI Quick Start** on the dashboard. In the dialog:

1. Describe your topic (e.g., "Machine learning fundamentals").
2. Choose a **depth**: Quick (~15 nodes), Balanced (~25 nodes), or Comprehensive (~40+ nodes).
3. Choose a **style**: Concise (key points), Detailed (rich explanations), or Academic (scholarly depth).
4. Optionally specify a **domain** to focus the AI's output (e.g., "computer science").
5. Click **Generate**. The AI builds a structured map with a root node, branches, and sub-branches.

### From a template
Click **Templates** to choose from pre-built structures like Brainstorm, Pros & Cons, Project Planning, SWOT Analysis, or Timeline. Templates give you a starting layout that you can modify freely.

### Import an existing map
Click **Import** and select a `.json` file previously exported from NeuroCanvas. The map is restored with all nodes, edges, and metadata intact.

## 3. Add and edit nodes

Once you are on the canvas:

- **Add a node**: Press `N` to activate the node tool, then click anywhere on the canvas. Or use the **Add Node** button in the bottom toolbar.
- **Edit node text**: Double-click a node to enter edit mode. Type your content and click outside or press `Escape` to confirm.
- **Move a node**: Click and drag it to a new position.
- **Connect nodes**: Press `C` to activate the connect tool, then drag from one node to another to create an edge.
- **Delete a node**: Select it and press `Delete` or `Backspace`.

The right sidebar shows properties for the selected node -- title, description, color, category, and AI suggestions. Use it to refine details without leaving the canvas.

## 4. Navigate the canvas

The canvas is infinite. Here is how to move around:

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| **Pan** | Hold `Space` and drag, or use the middle mouse button |
| **Zoom** | Scroll wheel, or `Cmd/Ctrl` + `+`/`-` |
| **Fit to screen** | Press `F` to zoom out and center all nodes in view |
| **Reset zoom** | Press `1` to return to 100% zoom |
| **Minimap** | A small overview in the corner shows your position. Click it to jump to a location. |

On touch devices, use two-finger pinch to zoom and two-finger drag to pan.

## 5. Use AI features

With a node selected:

- **Smart Expand** (`Cmd/Ctrl` + `E`): The AI analyzes the selected node in context and suggests child nodes. Accept individual suggestions or add them all at once.
- **Deep Expand**: In the AI Suggestions panel, click "Deep expand" to generate multi-level hierarchies from the selected node.
- **Generate Description**: The AI can write a summary and detailed description for any node based on its title and position in the map.

The AI Suggestions panel in the sidebar shows suggestion cards as they are generated. Each card includes a title, description, category, and relationship label. Click a card to add it to your map.

## 6. Save and access maps later

NeuroCanvas auto-saves your work. Every change is persisted to IndexedDB locally and, if you are signed in on web, synced to the server.

- The **auto-save indicator** in the top bar shows the last save time.
- Return to the **Dashboard** at any time to see all your maps, sorted by most recently edited.
- From the dashboard, click any map card to reopen it on the canvas.
- Use the **All Maps** page (accessible from the sidebar) for a full list with search and filtering.
