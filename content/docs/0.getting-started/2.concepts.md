# Concepts

NeuroCanvas is built around a small set of composable concepts. Understanding these will help you get the most out of the application.

## Maps

A **map** is the top-level container for your work. Each map has:

- A **title** and optional **description**.
- A collection of **nodes** and **edges**.
- **Metadata** like creation date, last modified time, and sync status.
- A **root node** that serves as the central topic.

You can have as many maps as you need. They are listed on the Dashboard and the All Maps page. Maps are stored locally in IndexedDB and, when signed in on web, synced to the server.

## Nodes

A **node** is an individual idea, topic, or piece of information placed on the canvas. Each node has:

- **Content**: The visible text label (the idea itself).
- **Position**: X and Y coordinates on the infinite canvas.
- **Size**: Width and height, adjustable manually or auto-sized to content.
- **Style**: Fill color, border color, border width, text color, font size, font weight, and shape (rounded, rectangle, circle, or diamond).
- **Type**: Text (default), image, icon, or note.
- **Metadata**: Optional description (AI-generated or manual), category, relationship to parent, and AI provenance data.

### Node hierarchy

Nodes exist in a loose hierarchy determined by edges:

- **Root node**: The central topic of the map. Visually distinct (teal fill, no border by default). Every map has exactly one.
- **Tier-1 nodes**: Direct children of the root. Main branches of the topic.
- **Tier-2+ nodes**: Children of tier-1 nodes and deeper. Sub-branches and details.

The hierarchy is not rigid. You can connect any node to any other node, creating lateral relationships alongside the tree structure.

### Node states

- **Selected**: Blue selection ring, properties visible in sidebar.
- **Locked**: Cannot be moved or edited until unlocked.
- **Collapsed**: Children are hidden to reduce visual clutter. Expand to reveal them again.

## Edges

An **edge** is a connection between two nodes. Edges represent relationships -- cause and effect, parent-child, association, dependency, or any link you define.

Each edge has:

- **Source** and **target** node IDs.
- **Style**: Color, width, and line pattern.
- **Label** (optional): A short text describing the relationship (e.g., "causes", "depends on", "part of").
- **Anchor points**: Edges connect to specific sides of nodes (top, right, bottom, left), with automatic routing to minimize crossings.

Edges are directional by default (source to target), though the visual rendering can be configured.

## Canvas

The **canvas** is the infinite 2D workspace where nodes and edges live. Unlike a page or a slide, the canvas has no boundaries. You can pan in any direction and zoom from a bird's-eye overview down to fine detail.

### Camera

The canvas is viewed through a virtual **camera** defined by three values:

- **X and Y position**: Where the camera is centered.
- **Zoom level**: How far in or out you are. Zoom ranges from about 10% (very far out) to 500% (very close).

Panning moves the camera position. Zooming changes the scale. The minimap shows your camera viewport as a highlighted rectangle on a scaled-down view of the entire map.

### Viewport culling

For performance, NeuroCanvas only renders nodes and edges that are visible in the current viewport. Nodes outside the view are culled and not drawn to the screen. This allows maps with hundreds or thousands of nodes to remain responsive.

## Semantic fields

**Semantic fields** are an AI-powered visualization layer that reveals hidden relationships between nodes based on their meaning -- not just their explicit connections.

When enabled, NeuroCanvas computes **embedding vectors** for each node's content using a transformer model (either in-browser via transformers.js or locally via ONNX on desktop). It then calculates **cosine similarity** between all pairs of visible nodes.

Pairs that exceed a configurable similarity threshold are connected by **field lines** -- curved, animated lines that flow between semantically related nodes. Key properties:

- **Line width** scales with similarity strength. Stronger relationships produce thicker lines.
- **Particles** flow along field lines at speeds proportional to similarity. High-similarity connections pulse faster.
- **Cluster glow** highlights regions of the canvas where multiple related nodes are concentrated.
- **Resonance pulses** fire when you select a node, radiating outward to highlight its semantic neighbors.

Semantic fields are distinct from edges. Edges are explicit connections you create. Semantic fields are implicit relationships the AI discovers. Together, they give you two complementary views of your map's structure.

## AI engine

The **AI engine** powers several features across NeuroCanvas. It uses a BYOK (Bring Your Own Key) model, meaning you connect your own API keys from supported providers.

### Supported providers

| Provider | Key features |
|----------|-------------|
| **OpenAI** | GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 Mini, GPT-4.1 Nano, o4-mini |
| **Anthropic** | Claude models |
| **Ollama** | Local models, no API key needed |
| **OpenRouter** | Access to many models through one API |
| **Custom** | Any OpenAI-compatible endpoint |

### AI capabilities

- **Map generation**: Build an entire map structure from a text description.
- **Smart expansion**: Generate contextually relevant child nodes for any selected node.
- **Deep expansion**: Multi-level hierarchical expansion that creates branches several levels deep.
- **Description generation**: Write summaries and detailed descriptions for nodes based on their title and map context.
- **Connection suggestions**: AI analyzes your map and suggests edges between nodes that should be linked.
- **Semantic embeddings**: Vector representations of node content used for similarity computation and semantic field visualization.

### Personas

You can configure **personas** that control the AI's behavior: system prompt, temperature, max tokens, and other generation parameters. Different personas suit different tasks -- a creative brainstorming persona uses higher temperature, while a structured academic persona uses lower temperature and longer outputs.
