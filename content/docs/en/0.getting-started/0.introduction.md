# Introduction

NeuroCanvas is a cross-platform mind mapping application that combines an infinite canvas with AI-powered tools to help you think, plan, and learn more effectively. Instead of working with static lists or rigid outlines, you build spatial maps of interconnected ideas -- and let AI help you discover relationships, generate structure, and fill in the gaps.

## Who is NeuroCanvas for?

- **Students** preparing for exams, writing research papers, or organizing lecture notes into visual structures they can actually remember.
- **Researchers** mapping out literature, tracking connections between theories, and exploring new lines of inquiry with AI-assisted expansion.
- **Knowledge workers** brainstorming projects, planning product features, documenting processes, or running strategic workshops.
- **Writers and creatives** building world maps, plotting story arcs, or breaking down complex topics into publishable outlines.
- **Anyone** who thinks better when they can see the big picture and zoom into details on demand.

## Key features

### Try for free (guest mode)
Start mapping immediately without creating an account. Guest mode gives you a single map on an infinite canvas to explore the core experience. When you are ready for AI features, sync, and unlimited maps, sign up to unlock everything.

### Infinite canvas
Work on an unbounded 2D workspace. Pan, zoom, and arrange ideas freely without hitting the edges of a page. The canvas adapts to your thinking -- not the other way around.

### AI map generation
Describe a topic in plain language and let NeuroCanvas build a structured mind map for you. Choose depth (quick overview, balanced, or comprehensive) and style (concise, detailed, or academic) to match your needs.

### Template gallery
Browse a curated gallery of community and system templates organized by category -- Education, Business, Creative, Planning, and Research. Use a template as-is, or let the AI adapt it to a new topic. You can also publish your own maps as reusable templates.

### Smart node expansion
Select any node and ask the AI to generate related subtopics. NeuroCanvas analyzes the context of your map -- the parent node, siblings, and existing structure -- to suggest relevant, non-redundant ideas.

### Semantic field visualization
Toggle the semantic field layer to see AI-computed similarity relationships rendered as animated field lines between related nodes. Particles flow along these lines proportional to similarity strength, giving you an at-a-glance view of how your ideas cluster.

### AI suggestions panel
A sidebar panel that surfaces context-aware suggestions as you work. Expand a node, deep-expand into multi-level hierarchies, or accept individual suggestion cards into your map with one click.

### Cross-platform sync
Your maps sync across devices when you are signed in. Changes propagate automatically, and conflict resolution handles cases where the same map is edited on multiple devices.

### Share and export
Share maps via link -- generate a shareable URL that others can view without an account. Export maps as JSON (for backup and re-import), PNG (for presentations and documents), or Markdown (for plain-text workflows). Export your entire account data as a single JSON download.

### Account security
Protect your account with two-factor authentication (TOTP), password breach checking via HaveIBeenPwned, and full data portability with one-click account export.

## Platform support

| Platform | Status | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Web** | Stable | Full feature set. Works in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge. |
| **Desktop** (Tauri) | Beta | Native app for macOS, Windows, Linux. Includes local ML inference for embeddings. Auth is bypassed -- maps are stored locally. |
| **Mobile** (Capacitor) | Beta | iOS and Android via Capacitor shell. Responsive layout with bottom tab bar and touch gestures. |

The web platform is the primary target and receives the most testing. Desktop and mobile builds share the same Nuxt 3 codebase but wrap it in platform-specific shells (Tauri 2 for desktop, Capacitor for mobile). Some features -- like local ONNX model inference -- are only available on desktop.

## What you can do with NeuroCanvas

- Try the canvas immediately as a guest -- no sign-up required.
- Create mind maps manually by placing and connecting nodes on the canvas.
- Generate entire maps from a text prompt using AI.
- Browse, use, and publish templates from the template gallery.
- Expand any node with AI-suggested subtopics.
- Visualize hidden relationships between ideas with semantic fields.
- Navigate large maps with the minimap, graph view, and fit-to-screen controls.
- Customize node appearance: colors, shapes, sizes, and categories.
- Use keyboard shortcuts for fast, mouse-free workflows.
- Share maps via link or export in multiple formats.
- Sync maps across devices with automatic conflict resolution.
- Secure your account with two-factor authentication.
- Export all your data for portability and backup.
